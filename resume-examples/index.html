Resume Examples

Resume guides by role.

ATS keywords, sample bullet templates, and tailoring guidance for the 12 highest-volume US job categories. Updated May 2026.

Resume Example

Software Engineer Resume

A software engineer resume has to survive a Greenhouse or Workday parser, then a recruiter scanning for stack keywords, then a hiring manager who wants concrete shipped systems.

Project Manager Resume

A project manager resume gets read for three things: the size of what you delivered (budget, headcount, timeline), the methodology you ran (Agile, Waterfall, hybrid, PMP), and the verifiable outcomes.

View guide → Resume Example

Data Analyst Resume

A data analyst resume is scanned for the literal stack — SQL, Python, Tableau, Power BI, Looker — and for measurable business impact.

View guide → Resume Example

Product Manager Resume

Product manager resumes are scanned for three signals: shipped products with users, business outcomes (revenue, retention, growth metrics), and the engineering and design context the candidate worked in.

View guide → Resume Example

Marketing Manager Resume

Marketing manager resumes are scored on channels owned, budgets managed, and quantified outcomes (CAC, LTV, MQLs, pipeline, brand metrics).

View guide → Resume Example

Sales Representative Resume

Sales rep resumes are scored almost entirely on quota attainment, deal size, and tenure.

View guide → Resume Example

Business Analyst Resume

Business analyst resumes get scored on process improvement, requirements gathering, stakeholder mapping, and the data-and-tooling side (SQL, BI, JIRA, process modeling).

View guide → Resume Example

UX Designer Resume

UX designer resumes get scored on tools (Figma, Sketch, design systems), shipped products, and research.

View guide → Resume Example

Customer Service Representative Resume

Customer service resumes face the highest application volume of any white-collar role — a single posting can pull 800+ applicants.

View guide → Resume Example

Accountant Resume

Accountant resumes are scored on the systems (NetSuite, QuickBooks, Sage, SAP), the certifications (CPA, CMA), the close cycle (monthly, quarterly, year-end), and the size of the books managed.

View guide → Resume Example

HR Manager Resume

HR manager resumes get scored on lifecycle scope (recruiting, onboarding, performance, benefits, comp, ER), HRIS/ATS systems (Workday, BambooHR, Greenhouse, Lever, ADP), compliance keywords (FLSA, ADA, FMLA, Title VII, HIPAA), and headcount managed.

View guide → Resume Example

Operations Manager Resume

Operations manager resumes are scored on what was owned (cost, throughput, headcount, regions, sites, P&L), the systems used to manage it (ERP, WMS, OMS, Lean tools), and the quantified improvements made.
