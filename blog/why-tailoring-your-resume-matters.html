Why Sending the Same Resume to Every Job is Costing You Interviews

You spent hours perfecting your resume. It looks clean, professional, covers all your experience. So you send it to 50 jobs and wait. Nothing. Maybe one callback out of 50 applications. The problem isnt your experience. Its your resume.

75% of resumes never reach a human

Most companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to filter resumes before a recruiter ever sees them. These systems scan your resume for keywords that match the job description. If your resume doesnt contain the right terms, it gets filtered out automatically.

When you send the same generic resume to every job, you are rolling the dice on whether your wording happens to match. For most applications, it doesnt.

The keyword problem

A job posting for a "Project Coordinator" and one for a "Program Manager" might describe the exact same role. But if your resume says "Project Coordinator" and the ATS is scanning for "Program Manager," you get filtered.

This isnt about lying on your resume. (If youre not sure how ATS filtering works, read how ATS systems actually work.) Its about matching the language the employer uses to describe skills you already have. Different companies call the same things by different names.

What tailoring actually means

Tailoring doesnt mean rewriting your resume from scratch for every application. It means:

  • Matching keywords from the job posting in your summary and bullet points
  • Reordering your bullets so the most relevant experience comes first
  • Adjusting your skills section to highlight what the employer is looking for
  • Rephrasing your experience using the same language as the job description

Your facts stay the same. The presentation changes to match. Finding the right keywords is key, and we cover that in the resume keywords that get interviews. The presentation changes to match what each employer is specifically looking for.

The math is simple

If you send 50 generic resumes and get 1 callback, thats a 2% response rate. If you tailor each resume and get 5 callbacks from 50 applications, thats 10%. Tailoring takes the same experience and makes it visible to the people who need to see it.

The problem was never your qualifications. It was the packaging.
