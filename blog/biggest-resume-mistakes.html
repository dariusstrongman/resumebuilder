5 Resume Mistakes That Make Hiring Managers Skip Yours

April 2026 · 5 min read

Hiring managers spend 6-7 seconds scanning a resume before deciding whether to keep reading. That means small mistakes have outsized consequences. Here are the five that cost the most interviews.

1. Generic professional summary

"Results-driven professional with proven track record of success seeking challenging opportunities." This tells the hiring manager nothing. It could describe anyone in any field. Your summary should state what you do, how long you have been doing it, and what specific skills you bring. Three sentences. Specific.

2. Listing duties instead of results

"Responsible for managing team" vs "Managed a team of 12 across 3 departments, reducing project delivery time by 20%." The first describes the job. The second describes what you did with it. Every bullet should answer the question: so what?

3. One resume for every application

Sending the same generic resume to every job is the single biggest reason for low callback rates. ATS systems filter based on keyword matching against the specific job description. A resume tailored to a "Data Analyst" posting will score completely differently than one written for a "Business Intelligence Analyst" role, even if you are qualified for both.

4. Wrong format

Two-column layouts, graphics, tables, and creative designs look great on screen but get destroyed by ATS parsers. Stick to a single column, standard fonts, and traditional section headers. Save the creativity for your portfolio.

5. Typos and inconsistent formatting

One typo might not kill your chances. But inconsistent date formats, random capitalization, and misaligned bullets tell the hiring manager you dont pay attention to details. If your resume has errors, they will assume your work does too.
